Associate Product Manager Assignment

021 Terminal Mode

A web-based trading terminal designed to stay out of the trader’s way while staying fully on their side. This prototype focuses on speed, clarity, low cognitive load, and customizable workflows for different trading styles.

1. Research & Point of View

Why Web Over Desktop?

021 should prioritize a web-based terminal because traders increasingly expect instant access across devices without downloads, version updates, or operating system dependencies. A browser-based terminal lowers onboarding friction and enables faster experimentation, iteration, and rollout of features. Modern browsers are already capable of handling real-time streaming, advanced charts, and multi-panel layouts. However, a lightweight desktop executable should still be considered later for power users who demand lower latency, multi-monitor optimization, dedicated memory usage, and native keyboard shortcuts. The desktop app should not be the primary experience initially. Instead, it should evolve from proven web workflows once user behavior and high-frequency trading requirements become clearer.

“Out of your way. On your side.”

For an intraday trader, this philosophy means the terminal should never interrupt momentum. No popups blocking charts, no unnecessary confirmations, no clutter competing for attention. The interface should anticipate intent and reduce reaction time. For swing traders and investors, it means clarity over noise. They should immediately understand portfolio exposure, risk, and trends without digging through menus. For beginners, the platform should quietly guide rather than overwhelm. Smart defaults, contextual education, and simplified workflows should make the product feel supportive instead of intimidating.

2. Features & Building Blocks

Core Building Blocks

  • Watchlist: Track instruments with quick visual movement indicators.
  • Advanced Chart: Multi-timeframe charting with indicators and drawing tools.
  • Order Panel: Fast buy/sell execution with quantity presets and risk controls.
  • Market Depth: Real-time bid/ask visibility and liquidity understanding.
  • Positions & Holdings: Live P&L, open positions, and portfolio overview.
  • Alerts: Price movement, volume spikes, and volatility notifications.
  • Workspace Presets: Personalized layouts for different trading styles.

What 021 Should Do Differently

Most trading terminals overwhelm users with density. 021 should instead focus on contextual visibility. Panels should intelligently expand or collapse based on the user’s current task. Example: when placing an order, the market depth and risk summary should automatically become more prominent. Another differentiator should be workflow memory. The terminal should remember behavior patterns and reopen exactly how the trader left it. One feature I would deliberately avoid is an overcrowded social trading feed. Competitors often add noisy sentiment streams that distract from execution quality. Trading terminals should optimize decisions, not addiction loops.

3. Workspace Presets

Scalper Mode

Large order panel, compact charts, one-click execution, keyboard shortcuts prioritized.

Swing Trader Mode

Expanded multi-timeframe charts, portfolio exposure tracking, reduced market noise.

Investor Mode

Long-term portfolio view, fundamentals, sector performance, and watchlist summaries.

Beginner Mode

Simplified workflows, guided order flow, educational tooltips, and low-risk defaults.

4. Comparison

Platform What Works What Doesn’t
Groww 9.15 Terminal Cleaner visual hierarchy, modern interface, easier onboarding for beginners. Feels simplified for active traders. Important execution controls are less discoverable under pressure.
Zerodha Kite Terminal Efficient order flow, lightweight performance, reliable execution experience. Dense information architecture and dated interaction patterns increase cognitive load for newer users.

What 021 Should Do Better

  • Adaptive layouts that prioritize context instead of static panels.
  • Remember exact workspace state across sessions automatically.
  • Reduce friction through keyboard-first workflows and low interruption design.

Final Product POV

The future of trading terminals is not more information. It is better prioritization. 021 should feel calm under pressure, responsive without being noisy, and intelligent without pretending to replace the trader.
